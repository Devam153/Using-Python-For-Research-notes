"The Silent Patient" is a psychological thriller novel written by Alex Michaelides. The story revolves around Alicia Berenson, a talented painter who seemingly has a perfect life until she shoots her husband Gabriel five times in the face and then never speaks another word. Following the murder, Alicia becomes completely mute and is committed to a secure psychiatric facility called The Grove. She communicates only through her art, particularly through a haunting self-portrait she creates shortly after the murder. The novel is narrated by Theo Faber, a psychotherapist who becomes fascinated by Alicia's case and is determined to help her find her voice again. The book is a gripping exploration of trauma, secrets, and the complexities of the human psyche, with a shocking twist that leaves readers questioning the nature of truth and perception.
